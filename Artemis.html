Artemis Missions

The Artemis missions are a series of complex lunar expeditions foor the purpose of returning humans to the moon, expand scientific exploration, and prepare for crewed missions to mars. the artemis missions began with an uncrewed test-flight to a lunar flyby then afterwards what potentially could be the next landing on the moon after 50 years.

Artemis I

The Space launch system (SLS) is Nasa's super heavy-lift rocket which is designed to send the orion spacecraft, Astronauts, and large cargo to the moon and beyond. the SLS is the most powerful rocket nasa has ever launched, providing more payload mass, volume, and more departure energy than any other rocket. The SLS is designed to be evolvable, meaning newer variants are currently being designed and will be capable of deep space travelling.

About Orion

The orion spacecraft is nasa's deepspace crew vehicle designed to carry astronauts to and from the moon and return them safely to earth. The orion can support 4 crew (As in Artemis II) beyond the low earth orbit and is launched atop the SLS rocket as part of the artemis missions.
